Photography Portfolio

Photography Work

For my photography work I decided to focus on modern architecture and buildings. This page consists of six different images on that topic, where I have used composition techniques such as the rule of thirds, leading lines and reflections.

Image One

This is a photo of the back of UA92. I think this is a strong image due to the contrast between the red brick and the atmospheric clouds. I also like the use of leading lines and symmetry that take you from the bottom of the building up to the sky. Every image other than this one was shot on a Fujifilm X-T3, however I decided to shoot this particular image on my iPhone 11 because the lens on the phone is wider than the DSLR. If I used the DSLR for this photograph, then I would have had to stand much further back to fit all of the red brick in, making the picture less appealing aesthetically.

Image Two

This photo was taken as I was walking towards Media City. I like the exposure in this as it has tints of blue as well as tints of yellow from the sun. I like the pattern that the angle of the shot created. In addition, I think this is a successful image because it shows the full length of the bridge, while maintaining strong leading lines.

Image Three

I think that this is a successful photo because it takes you from a bridge, to a set of modern buildings and is also exposed well.

Image Four

This image is succesful because the focal point is the modern structure on the bridge. It is clearly in the rule of thirds and is also being lit up by the sun, making it pop that bit more. If I had stood on the other side of the bridge for the same photo, then it would've taken the architecture away from the viewers eyes as the focal point would become the sun, with the bridge silhoutted in the foreground.

Image Five

This is an image where I decided to have the sun facing the camera. I purposely used a low aperture for this picture, to get the rays of light coming from the sun. It is also in the rule of thirds which adds to the overall image. In the mid to lower third, there are multiple modern buildings. I like the way they are complemented by the colours of the sky.

Image Six

This image clearly shows symmetry by the use of reflections in the water. I think that makes the photo a good one because the building stands out more. In addition, I like the way the front of the building has sunlight being pushed onto it, while the rear is in the shade. In my opinion, this makes the contrast of the photo look pleasing to the eye.
